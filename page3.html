MY ONLINE PORTFOLIO

Characters in Noli Me Tangere
Juan Crisostomo Ibarra y Magsalin
- Ibarra is a young, wealthy mestizo who has recently returned to the Philippines from studying from 7 years study from Europe.
- He is smart, well-respected, and very idealistic.
Maria Clara de los Santos
- She is a woman of high social status and is believed to be the Father Damaso's goddaughter as well as the daughter of Capitan Tiago.
- She is truly Father Damaso's biological child, the result of their scandalous relationship with Capitan Tiago's wife.
Father Damaso
- Father Damaso is an elderly, ruthlessly corrupt and Spanish priest who has spent almost two decades residing among the native Filipinos.
- He has spent all that time among them, but the years have done nothing to endear him or give him any sympathy for his "flock".
- He is profoundly prejudiced, spiteful, and vengeful, and he has no problem using his immense influence to destroy people who have wronged him.
Father Salvi
- Salvi is depicted as a manipulative and scheming friar who uses his influence to further his own interests and maintain his position of power.
- Father Salvi's unrequited love for Maria Clara, the novel's heroine, creates a central romantic conflict and adds depth to his character, emphasizing the theme of forbidden love in the story.
Captain Tiago (Dong Santiago de los Santos)
- Captain Tiago is a wealthy Filipino landowner. He is well-respected in the community and holds a prominent social position.
- Captain Tiago is the loving father of Maria Clara. He is deeply concerned about her well-being and happiness.
Sisa
- Sisa is a deeply tragic character who is the mother of Basilio and Crispin. She is depicted as mentally unstable and suffers greatly due to the abuses and injustices in society.
- Sisa serves as a symbol of maternal suffering and the devastating impact of colonial oppression on families. Her character highlights the human toll of the harsh conditions faced by ordinary Filipinos during the Spanish colonial era.
Crispin
- Crispin is a young boy who becomes orphaned and is left in a vulnerable position. He faces hardship and abuse, which reflects the broader themes of societal injustices in the story.
- Crispin was falsely accused of stealing money from the church, which sets off a chain of events that leads to his suffering and ultimately his tragic fate in the story.
Elias
- Elias is a mysterious and enigmatic character in the novel who is deeply involved in the Philippine revolution against Spanish colonial rule. He is known for his bravery and dedication to the cause of liberation.
- Elias becomes an important ally to Crisostomo Ibarra, as they share a common goal of seeking justice and reform in the Philippines under Spanish colonial rule.
